Floating Copper + Iron Series

Testing calcined alumina and zircopax additions to a floating iron + copper glaze

This series explores variations of the Old Forge Floating Copper recipe with increasing iron additions (0.5%, 1%, 1.5%, 4%), maintaining 1% copper. Each subseries examines the effects of adding zircopax or a combination of calcined alumina and zircopax in equal proportions to observe changes in color and texture.

Calcined Alumina Variation with 0.5% Iron

This subseries uses 1% copper and 0.5% iron, with equal parts Calcined Alumina and Zircopax introduced in increasing quantities from 1% to 8%. Each test shows tiles on black and white backgrounds.

Conclusion

The addition of calcined alumina and zircopax can affect the glaze in various ways, from opacity changes to color shifts. The combination with copper and iron allows for controlled color development in floating blue glazes across different iron levels. The full series progresses to 8% of each additive.
