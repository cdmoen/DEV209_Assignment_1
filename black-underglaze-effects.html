Black Underglaze
Effects

Using 'Best Black' 6600 Mason Stain as a black underglaze beneath various glazes.
Discovering unexpected interactions and surface effects in cone 6 oxidation firing.

Experimenting with 'Best Black' mason stain to create a black underglaze for porcelain pottery can dramatically impact the aesthetics of certain glazes. This technique involves applying the black underglaze beneath various glaze layers, leading to unexpected surface effects, color variations, and textural changes during cone 6 oxidation firing. However, the results are not universally positive - extensive testing is required to identify which glazes benefit from this treatment, as some combinations may produce muddy or undesirable outcomes while others reveal beautiful, complex interactions that enhance the pottery's visual appeal.

Effect Variations

The following variations showcase some of the more successful glaze combinations tested with the black underglaze made from 'Best Black' mason stain. Each pair of images shows the same glaze applied over plain white porcelain (left) and over a layer of black underglaze (right), demonstrating the dramatic effects that can occur.
